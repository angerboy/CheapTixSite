CheapTix

EECS 349: Northwestern University

Matthew Ehinger, Eduardo Bermudez, Lauren Denaut

Contact email: matthewehinger2016@u.northwestern.edu

Problem:

With the advent of automated ticket purchasing came an introduction of a new market: the resale ticket market. Unlike standard venue ticket purchasing, ticket prices on resale sites such as StubHub are volatile, and will change from day to day. This introduces the problem of deciding when to purchase resale tickets, when you are not sure if the price will go up or down in the future. This is the problem that we set out to solve.

Solution:

We tracked a collection of concerts in different cities, and recorded information about them, including ratio of lowest resale price to retail price, days until performance, venue capacity, and city. Each instance of our data set has a binary classification of either ‘wait’, or ‘buy’, indicating whether or not the price will go up, or down/stay the same. Using this data we conducted a three nearest neighbor learning algorithm to predict the binary classification.
